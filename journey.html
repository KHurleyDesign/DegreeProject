User Journeys

After creating the personas, I analyzed a key issue brought up in the interview and created a user journey to examine before, during, and after the issue. My user journeys encompass before, during, and after the key issue — considerations include the thoughts and feelings of the user, their actions and decision making process.

  • Robert
  • Martha
  • Henry

Robert

User Goal

Robert wants to be able to connect with family, friends, and old classmates. As well as staying up-to-date on town news.

Discovery

Roberts extended family recommends Facebook to him. He sees how the app would be useful.

User Goal

Robert wants to stay connected with extended family and friends.

Account Setup

Robert downloads Facebook and sets up an account. Account set up goes quickly and is easy for him.

Friending People

Robert wants to friend his immediate family, extended family, as well as some of his old hunting buddies. Robert is able to find most of the people successfully, but has trouble with a few that he knows have Facebook accounts.

User Goal

Robert wants to friend the people he knows.

Real Friend Request

Robert receives a friend request from someone that he knows from high school. He accepts the friend request.

Thoughts and Feelings

Robert is exited to see what his old friend is up to.

Bot Friend Request

Robert receives a friend request from someone he does not know or remember. Not wanting to offend the person if he does actually know them, he accepts the friend request anyway.

Challenges

Not being able to distinguish a real friend request from a bot.

Opportunities for Innovation

Better bot prevention, education about bots, and warning when the account is not in your friend group.

Discovery of Bot

Robert asks his daughter a question about the app. The daughter sees the unknown friend and upon further examination, the "friend" has sexual photos attached to the account. The daughter confronts Robert.

Thoughts and Feelings

Robert is angry and embarrassed.

Deleting Account

Robert, upset and embarrassed, deletes his account.

Thoughts and Feelings

Robert is frustrated and disapointed with Facebook.

User Goal

Robert does not want to be associated with Facebook anymore.

Martha

User Goal

Martha wants to be able to use Facebook to communicate with family that does not live within visiting distance.

Discovery

Martha's daughter mentions to her that she should join Facebook, because it would be easier to communicate and they would be able to share photos and videos. Martha is hesitant, but says that she will look into it.

Challenges

Martha feels uncomfortable around technology.

Reaserch

Martha mentions the conversation to her granddaughter, who then shows Martha her own account and explains why it might be useful. Martha agrees to sign up.

Challenges

Martha has heard negative things about Facebook, she thinks that it is where a lot of people air out their "dirty laundry".

Thoughts and Feelings

Martha is apprehensive about the process but is willing to try and excited about the possibilities.

Account Setup

Martha and her granddaughter sit down to start the account. She does not have an email, so they must set one up first. They then go through the setup process, adding user information and a profile photo.

First Comment

Martha wants to comment on a photo of her grandchild that her daughter posted.

Challenges

Martha has difficulty with the comment interface, to the point of being unsure if her comments even send. Lack of internet language etiquette might cause her comment to be percieved as confusing or inappropriate.

Opportunities for Innovation

Prompt that a user could opt into that explains the interface and gives examples of tone/language used.

First Post

Martha wants to post about an outing that she has with her granddaughter to her friends.

Facebook Messenger

Martha decides that she want to message her daughter through Facebook and is prompted with a new interface for the messaging system.

Henry

User Goal

Henry wants to disseminate information about practice and games to players and parents.

Discovery

Henry has been posting information for his baseball team on his personal page. He learns from a fellow teacher about Facebook Pages and decided to see if it is something his team would be interested in.

Creation of Separate Page

After talking with his players and their parents Henry decides to create the page for the team. After some deliberation he labels it as a community organization page.

Customer Goal

Henry wants a space that he can share information with his players and parents that is separate from his personal Facebook page.

Challenges

Deciding what type of page it is based on Facebook's options.

Posting an Update

After a heavy rainfall, Henry decides to cancel practice to preserve the field. He posts about it on the page.

Using the Poll Feature

Henry wants players and parents input on date selection for the senior celebration. He creates a poll on the page.

Challenges

Getting all the players and parents to vote on the poll.

Opportunities for Innovation

Notification and reminder system for the poll encouraging page followers to participate.

Creating a Facebook Group

Henry decides that the Facebook page is too public and anyone can access it. He wants to create a group that is only for current members and players.

Sharing the Group

Henry adds his assistant coach to the group and invites his players and their parents.

Thinking and Feeling

Henry finds this process tedious and wishes there was a better way to share the group.
